HOW IT WORKS

Welcome to the world of Artificial Waves! The future of surfing and watersports has finally arrived. Here, at waves.guide, you will find everything you need to know about the fast-evolving world of man-made waves, from the basics of what an artificial wave is, to the latest advancements in wave technology. Whether you're a seasoned surfer looking for the next big wave, or just starting to explore this exciting new world, we've got you covered. Discover the closest artificial wave parks to you, compare prices (per minute surfing) and features, and learn about the science behind how artificial waves are made. And the best part? You can easily book your session at these state-of-the-art wave parks, all from the comfort of your own home, right here at waves.guide. Not only will you have an unforgettable experience, but you'll also be supporting a good cause. Half of the commission we receive from your bookings at our affiliate partners will be donated to a foundation dedicated to making artificial waves more environmentally sustainable. Get ready to catch the wave of the future, and join us as we dive into the amazing world of Artificial Waves!

How it works Waves.guide shows a top menu of ‘filter buttons’ just below the search box

These buttons represent filters:

  1. All

    World Map, (This is the Default filter selection. The horizontal bar below the filter button means: filter = selected.)

  2. Near-me

  3. ~

    Rolling

  4. S

    Standing

  5. R

    River

  6. TT

    Indoor

  7. [-

    Open

  8. [

    Planned

  9. [-]

    Closed

  10. K

    Kids, means: waves that are suitable for kids younger than or 8 years old. (Please check the exact rules and regulations with the wave owner.)

Click on a filter-button: - The system shows the marker icons on the map corresponding to the filter-button pressed.

Click the top right button: - The system shows the filtered wavelocations in a swipeable list or on the map.

Select multiple filters (purple icon) - Multiple filters are selected, like: open, near-me, indoor, rolling
